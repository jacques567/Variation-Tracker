# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Self-employed and small-team building contractors (electricians, builders, kitchen fitters, etc.) in the UK, tracking variations (change orders) against a fixed-price job. They use this mid-job, often on a phone, standing on site or in a van — not at a desk. Low patience for friction; needs to be legible in bright daylight and usable one-handed.

## Product Purpose
VarTracker lets a contractor log a job's contract value, record variations (extra work/cost changes) as they come up, get the client to sign off on each variation via a shareable link, and export an invoice reflecting the signed total. Success = no variation goes unbilled because it wasn't captured or signed off in the moment.

## Brand Personality
Trustworthy, no-nonsense, fast. Should feel like a tool a tradesperson respects — not consumer-cute, not enterprise-heavy. Think "the paperwork app that doesn't feel like paperwork."

## Anti-references
Not a generic SaaS dashboard template (no gradient hero cards, no bento grids, no decorative illustrations). Not over-designed fintech (no glassmorphism, no gradient text). This is a working tool used outdoors and mid-task, not a marketing surface.

## Design Principles
- Legible over decorative — numbers (money, dates) must be scannable at a glance, one-handed, on a small screen, in daylight.
- Never let content dictate a wider viewport than the device — real job names, addresses, and amounts are unpredictable length and must never overflow.
- Mobile is the primary surface, not an afterthought breakpoint — design mobile-first, then expand.
- Every screen has a clear next action (add variation, share link, export) that stays reachable without hunting.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
No stated WCAG target yet. Should hold up in bright outdoor light (sufficient contrast) and remain usable one-handed at common phone widths (320–430px).
