Privacy Policy

Last updated: 27 April 2026  ·  Effective immediately

Variation Tracker is operated by WildFireCo (trading as WildFire), a UK-registered company. This policy explains how we collect, use, and protect personal data in connection with the Variation Tracker service at vartracker.com.

Who we are

Variation Tracker is a trading name of WildFireCo (company number 17181791), registered in England and Wales with Companies House. We are the data controller for all personal data processed through vartracker.com.

You can contact us about privacy matters at admin@vartracker.com.

Who this policy covers

This policy covers two groups of people whose personal data we process:

  • Contractors — individuals and sole traders who register for a Variation Tracker account to manage their jobs and variations.
  • Clients — individuals whose details contractors enter into the platform (name, email, phone, address) and who may be sent a variation notice for e-signature. Clients do not have accounts with us.

How long we keep your data

  • Account data — deleted within 90 days of account cancellation, unless a longer retention period is required by law.
  • Job, variation, and invoice records — retained for 6 years from the date of the last transaction, in line with the UK Limitation Act 1980 and standard financial record-keeping requirements.
  • Signature records (including IP address) — retained for 6 years from the date of signing, as they form part of the contractual record for the variation.
  • Photos — retained for the same period as the associated variation record. Deleted when the parent job is deleted by the contractor.

Who we share your data with

We do not sell personal data. We share data only with the following third-party processors, each under a Data Processing Agreement:

  • Supabase — our database, authentication, and file storage provider. See supabase.com/privacy.
  • Stripe — our payment processor. We do not store card details. See stripe.com/gb/privacy.

We may also disclose personal data where required to do so by law, court order, or regulatory authority.

International data transfers

Supabase and Stripe may process data outside the UK or European Economic Area. Where this occurs, we rely on appropriate transfer mechanisms — including Standard Contractual Clauses and UK International Data Transfer Agreements — to ensure your data receives equivalent protection.

You can request details of the specific transfer mechanisms in place by contacting us at admin@vartracker.com.

Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including row-level security on all database tables, private access-controlled file storage, secure session management via Supabase Auth, and HTTPS enforced across all pages.

In the event of a personal data breach, we will notify the Information Commissioner's Office within 72 hours where required, and affected individuals without undue delay where the breach poses a high risk to their rights and freedoms.

Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, port, and object to the processing of your personal data. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at admin@vartracker.com. We will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk.

Note for clients (non-account holders)

If you have received a variation notice from a contractor who uses Variation Tracker, your name, contact details, and signature have been processed by us as part of providing the service to that contractor. You have the same rights as described above. Contact us at admin@vartracker.com with details of the contractor or job, and we will assist you.
